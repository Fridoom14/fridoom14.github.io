I am an aspiring data professional with a background in Economic Development, currently building a strong foundation in data analysis and applied data science. I am particularly interested in using data to understand real-world problems and support better decision-making across industries.

My academic training in economics has shaped how I approach data — not just as numbers, but as representations of behaviour, systems, and policy impact. During my experience as a research assistant at Indonesia’s financial regulatory authority (OJK), I contributed to the development of a supervisory reporting system (OBOX), where I was exposed to data-driven monitoring and the role of information systems in financial oversight.

In parallel, I have been developing practical skills in data analysis, including data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and basic machine learning. I enjoy working on projects that combine analytical thinking with real-world relevance, particularly those related to digital behaviour, financial systems, and risk patterns.

I also bring experience from the social impact sector, where I contributed to programme development and initiatives related to accessibility and inclusion. This has strengthened my interest in using data and technology not only for efficiency, but also for broader societal impact.

With a broad foundation in data science and a focused interest in economic and financial contexts, I aim to grow into a data professional who can bridge data, domain knowledge, and practical insights.
